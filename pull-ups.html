Description

This tutorial will guide you from 0 to 10 pull-ups.

It's divided into 3 sections based on your current level. I highly recommend reading through the whole tutorial
if you're not yet able to do 10 pull-ups but if you're short on time, everything is also summarized in the Beginner Plan.

From 0-1 Pull-Up

If you can't do a single pull-up yet â€” no problem. You'll get there soon. The first thing you should work on is grip strength. Aim to hold a dead hang for about 10-15 seconds. (If you can't do that yet, check out this tutorial: link to tutorial .)
Once you can hang for that long, start practicing these three exercises:

1. Negative Pull-Ups

Grab the bar, jump up or step onto a platform to get your chin over the bar, then slowly lower yourself down without touching the ground. (All exact sets and reps can be found in the beginner plan.)

2. Australian Rows (Inverted Rows)

This is an easier version of a pull-up. Use a lower bar so your feet stay on the ground.

3. Scapula pull-ups

While in a dead hang position push your scapula down so you activate your back muscles.

4. Dead Hangs

Continue doing dead hangs regularly to improve your grip and shoulder stability.

Bonus Exercise:

If you have access to one, you can also use a resistance band to assist your pull-ups. It's a great way to practice the full range of motion while still building strength.

From 1-3 Pull-Ups

Now that you can do your first pull-up, great job! You've passed the hardest part.
Here 's how to keep progressing:

1. Keep doing exercise from before

Do all the exercise mentioned before.

2. Regular Pull-Ups

Start your workout by attempting as many pull-ups asa you can. The form doesn't have to be perfect, but make sure it's controled and safe.

3. Chin over bar-holds

Get in a top position of a pull-up (like in a negative rep), but instead of lowering down, hold yourself at the top as long as possible.
You can also try holding in other parts of pull-up range, like halfway down.

Bonus Tip:

If you want to you can do all the exercise mentioned above with diferent kinds of grips.
I recommend this beacuse you will have more fun beacuse of variation.

From 3 - 10 Pull-Ups

This is, in my opinion, the most fun part of the journey.
To reach 10 pull-ups, keep doing the previous exercises, but with less volume. Now, focus more on actual pull-ups.

Here's a powerful method:

Pick a number (like 10 pull-ups) and don't end the workout until you complete all 10, no matter how many sets it takes. Each set should be to failure or close to failure.
This method works best if you can train multiple times per day, but if that's not possible, just do more reps in a single session.
Gradually increase the total number of pull-ups each week or every two weeks until you hit 10 in one set.
All recommended reps, sets, and rest times are available in the beginner plan.

What after?

After you've managed to do 10 pull-ups, you can choose what you want to focus on next. That could be muscle-ups, the front lever, or simply continuing to improve your pull-up strength. All of this is explained in more detail in the Beginner Plan.
