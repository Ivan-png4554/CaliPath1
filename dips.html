Description

This tutorial will guide you from 0 to 12 dips.

It's divided into 3 sections based on your current level. I highly recommend reading through the whole tutorial
if you're not yet able to do 12 Dips but if you're short on time, everything is also summarized in the Beginner Plan.

From 0 to 1 Dip

If you can't do a single dip, you're just like I was when I started — and now I can do 30 reps, so you can too.
But first, as with any exercise, you need to work your way up slowly to that magical first rep.
Here are the key exercises to get there:

1. Top hold

Simply hold yourself in the top position of the dip for as long as you can. This builds the necessary stability and starting strength.

2. Scapular Dips

While holding the top position, lower and raise your shoulders in a controlled motion. This is a fantastic exercise for building shoulder control and will help you later with advanced skills like the planche.

3. Negative Dips

If you've read the pull-up or push-up tutorials, you already know this drill. Negatives are king for building strength in any movement.
Start at the top position of the dip, then slowly lower yourself down until your arms are fully bent.

From 1 to 5 Dips

Once you've nailed your first proper dip with good form (as shown at the start), you're ready for the next step.
Here's what you should focus on next:

1. Keep Doing the Previous Exercises

The exercises mentioned earlier are not only great for building strength, but also for improving form and creating a better mind-muscle connection. Don't drop them — they'll make everything easier.

2. Regular Dips

If you can only do one dip, that's perfectly fine. With time and consistent training, your reps will naturally go up. Just keep practicing, and the progress will come gradually.

From 5 to 12 Dips

Once you can do 5 dips, you can proudly say you're getting good at them — but there's still work to do before you hit 12 reps. Here's the method that personally helped me get there:

The Method:

Choose a target number of total dips — for example, 30 dips. Don't finish your workout until you've completed all 30 reps, no matter how many sets it takes.
Rest as long as you need between sets, but keep going until you hit your target. Over time, increase that total number gradually, and your max reps will rise along with it.
Eventually, you'll reach 12 dips (or maybe even more).
