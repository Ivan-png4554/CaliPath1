Push-Ups

CaliPath

Description

This tutorial will guide you from 0 to 30 push-ups.

It's divided into 3 sections based on your current level. I highly recommend reading through the whole tutorial
if you're not yet able to do 30 push-ups but if you're short on time, everything is also summarized in the Beginner Plan.

From 0 to 1 Push-Up

If you can't do a single push-up yet — don't worry. Everyone starts somewhere. In fact, I'd even say that those who start with less strength often end up understanding their progress better because they had to build it step by step.
Here's how to achieve your first push-up:

1. Plank

Start by simply holding a plank. Keep your hands shoulder-width apart and hold the position as long as you can. This builds core and shoulder stability.

2. Incline Push-Ups

Find a surface higher than the ground — like a table, bed, or a lower bar. The higher the surface, the easier the push-up will be. Gradually lower the height as you get stronger.

3. Negative Push-Ups

Just like with pull-ups, negative push-ups are great for building strength. Get into a push-up position and slowly lower yourself all the way to the floor. Repeat for as many reps as you can.

From 1 to 10 Push-Ups

Now it's time to focus more on actual push-ups — and you'll quickly reach 30!
Here's how to go from 1 to 10 push-ups:

1. Standard Push-Ups

Do as many clean push-ups as you can. If you can only do one, that's fine. On your second or third rep, at least try to control the negative (lowering) part. Over time, your strength will grow, and you'll be able to do more.

2. Push-Ups From the Floor

Start by lying on the ground, then push yourself up into the top position of a push-up. This exercise is great for improving form — so focus on pushing up with the best form you can manage.

3. Keep Doing the Earlier Exercises

Continue practicing the exercises from the previous section, but now prioritize these two new ones. Do the earlier ones near the end of your workout as accessory work.

From 10 to 30 Push-Ups

Here's how to progress from 10 to 30 push-ups effectively:

1. Decline Push-Ups

This is a more challenging variation of the regular push-up. Find an elevated surface (like a bench or a bed), place your feet on it, and perform push-ups in that position. You'll notice it's much harder — and great for building strength.

2. Improve Your Form

If your form isn't solid yet, take time to fix it. Nobody wants to be the person claiming 50 push-ups when they all look sloppy.
The best exercise for improving form is the negative push-up.
Instead of doing reps to failure, focus on fewer reps with perfect technique. Quality over quantity!

3. Pyramid Sets

This is the most important method at this stage.
Do one set of maximum push-ups (let's say you hit 12). On the next set, do one less — so 11, then 10, and so on. Keep going until you can't maintain the pattern anymore.
This gradually builds both endurance and strength.
